# Brand Voice

## Current Voice
The homepage now leans toward:
- B2B parent-brand/product language
- strategic ecosystem messaging
- clearer visitor-facing explanations of where to start and what each layer does

## Desired Voice
- Clear
- practical
- structured
- credible
- concrete
- product-aware

## What The Voice Should Emphasize
- DII helps founders and operators move from confusion to structure.
- The company builds connected systems, not disconnected apps.
- Benefits should be stated in plain business language.
- Differences between founder planning, visa support, operations, warehouse control, and finance should be explicit when the products appear side by side.
- The site should feel like a live company homepage, not a planning document.
- The copy should be explicit about what is live today versus what is still expanding next.
- Trust should be reinforced with factual cues, not inflated claims or invented social proof.
- Contact and enquiry copy should reduce friction by making the next step explicit and easy to act on.
- Contact copy should make the first email feel like a useful starting brief, not a dead-end handoff.
- Above-the-fold copy should help visitors recognise themselves quickly, then point them directly to the closest route without making them decode the page first.

## What To Avoid
- Talking to the site owner in live copy
- "This section can become" framing on production pages
- vague "innovation" language with no concrete outcome
- internal TODO notes visible to visitors
